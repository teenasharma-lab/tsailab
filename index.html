A letter

I didn't set out to be the person who holds three brands together at once.

It happened gradually — an agency desk first, juggling twenty-odd clients at SimplePlan, learning what actually moves a number versus what just looks good in a deck. Then, slowly, founders started asking me to stay closer. Not as an outside vendor, but as someone inside the room — briefing the creative, managing the agency, writing the copy, watching the ROAS.

I still think about marketing the way I did when I started: a brand is a story, and a story that doesn't sell is just decoration. So I split my attention between the two things that usually pull marketers in opposite directions — the voice a brand uses, and the number it needs to hit — and I've tried to make sure neither one apologises for the other.

Today that means coordinating campaigns, briefing creative, and sitting close enough to founders in lean D2C teams that there's no real gap between strategy and execution. I'm still drawn to the same question I started with: how do you build something that sounds like itself and still performs.
